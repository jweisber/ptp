Probability Through Paradox

7.2 R is for Terror

Where is the letter R more likely to occur in an English word?

  1. The first position
  2. The third position

Most people say (1): first position. But (of course) third position is actually more likely.

  • (That’s not something you can just figure out by logic; you have to run a search on a dictionary and count.)

So why do people tend to think first position is more likely?

  • Kahneman and Tversky hypothesized that people rely on an “availability” heuristic.
    • It’s easier to think of words that begin with R—they’re more readily available in memory.
    • The easier it is to imagine or recall an example of something, the more probable it seems.

Question: if our reasoning is distorted by heuristics like availability, how do those distortions affect our society?

Psychologist Joshua D. Foster gave one take on this question:

the 2009 budget for homeland security (the folks that protect us from terrorists) will likely be about $50 billion. Don’t get us wrong, we like the fact that people are trying to prevent terrorism, but even at its absolute worst, terrorists killed about 3,000 Americans in a single year. And less than 100 Americans are killed by terrorists in most years. By contrast, the budget for the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (the folks who protect us on the road) is about $1 billion, even though more than 40,000 people will die this year on the nation’s roads. —Joshua D. Foster, Psychology Today
