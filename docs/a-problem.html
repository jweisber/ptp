Probability Through Paradox

2.5 A Problem?

If you flip a coin 100 times and it lands heads every time, shouldn’t you conclude that the next toss will almost certainly be heads?

  • And doesn’t that mean the gambler’s “fallacy” isn’t really a fallacy?

Yes and no:

  • Yes, you should conclude the next toss will probably be heads.
  • No, this doesn’t present a problem for the gambler’s fallacy.
    • You’ve learned the coin isn’t fair!
    • Only if you know a process is fair, yet neglect that fact, are you guilty of the Gambler’s fallacy.
