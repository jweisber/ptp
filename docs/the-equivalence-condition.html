5.3 The Equivalence Condition

The paradox rests on two general principles: Nicod’s Criterion and…

The Equivalence Condition

If two hypotheses are equivalent, then whatever confirms one of them confirms the other as well.

What does “equivalent” mean here?

  • It’s the standard notion of logical equivalence, which you may have encountered in other philosophy (or math) classes.
    • If \(H\) is true then \(H'\) must be true, and if \(H'\) is true then \(H\) must be true.

To defuse the paradox, you might reject The Equivalence Condition.

  • But that’s a pretty big bullet to bite.
